THE MISSION

As students and faculty in a STEM field, we've brushed against the limitations of data-driven research, including the inequities that are pervasive throughout big tech and academia.

We created Free the Data to say fuck you to these limitations.

Our goal is to to tell stories that use data in compelling, interactive, and visual ways, while breaking stereotypes about who can do data analysis and what proper research topics look like. Collectively, the content that we showcase reflects the diversity of our interests and our shared commitment to driving data in more ethical, engaging, and empowering directions.

Free the Data joins a growing movement that calls on academics, journalists, scientists, and anyone else interested in data justice to challenge what it means to work with data. If you're new to data justice, you might start by checking out our Data 101 series. This primer covers some of the essential concepts that have helped guide this project.

Otherwise, we invite you to explore our articles, say hi to us on Twitter, or reach out to us about contributing to the project!
