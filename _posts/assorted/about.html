THE MISSION.

Free the Data joins a growing movement that calls on academics, journalists, scientists, and anyone else interested in data justice to challenge what it means to work with data. As students and faculty in a STEM field, we've brushed against the limitations of data-driven research and the inequities that are endemic to big tech and academia alike.

We created Free the Data to say fuck you to these limitations.

Our goal is not only to name the problems that we encounter at the intersection of data and society, but to explore how we can do our part to address these problems. We invite others to join us, to tell data stories in compelling, interactive, visual ways, while also breaking stereotypes about who can do data analysis and what proper research topics look like.

Across the site you'll find articles and resources that engage with topics such as health and medicine, politics, pop culture, and social justice. Collectively, these projects center our agency as researchers; they reflect the diversity of our interests, as well as our shared commitment to driving data in more ethical, engaging, and empowering directions.

If you're new to data justice, you might start by checking out our Data Justice 101 article . This primer covers some of the essential figures and concepts that have helped inspire this project.
