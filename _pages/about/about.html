Who we are

As students and faculty in a STEM field, we're unfortunately well-acquainted with the limitations of data-driven research, including the inequities that are endemic to it. Free the Data says fuck you to these limitations and demands research that drives data in more ethical, engaging, and empowering directions.

Across the site, you'll find essays and visual projects that use data in compelling and interactive ways, while breaking stereotypes about who can do data analysis and what proper research topics look like. Our projects engage fields such as information science, critical data studies, science of science, feminism, and communication. We hope you'll stick around and join us in saying ENOUGH WITH BIG TECH.

— the Free the Data team

Projects →

Infographics, dashboards, graphs and #DataArt: If we can find a way to visualize something, we will. If it works, we'll share it here. (And if it doesn't, we'll probably share it here anyway. F*CK perfectionism.)

Resources →

We believe in smashing black boxes that make it hard to access, analyze, or afford data. We strive to help make data science more empowering by providing resources on data science and data justice.

Data 101 →

In our Data 101 series, we share what we've learned about the process of writing scripts, breaking things, identifying problems, and experiencing the joy of fixing them.
